# Semiconductor ETF Deep Dive 2026: SMH vs SOXX vs China Chip ETFs

**By ETF Bridge Research** | July 1, 2026 | 10 min read

---

Semiconductors are the single most concentrated bet in the global equity market. They power AI, EVs, smartphones, defense systems, cloud computing, and the entire digital economy. And in 2026, semiconductor ETFs are delivering some of the most extreme returns — and the widest performance dispersion — of any fund category.

China's niche semiconductor equipment ETFs have returned **over 170%** in the first half of 2026. The original US semiconductor ETF — VanEck's SMH — is up roughly 64% YTD. And iShares' SOXX, with its broader, more equipment-heavy mandate, has surged **87%** in the same period.

If you own an S&P 500 ETF, you already have ~8% in Nvidia alone. The question is whether you need more. This article compares the three major categories of semiconductor ETFs — US concentrated (SMH), US diversified (SOXX), and China onshore chip ETFs — to help you decide where, if anywhere, semiconductor exposure fits beyond the broad indices.

## SMH: The Original Chip Bet

Launched in 2011, SMH was the first semiconductor ETF — and for years, the only one. It tracks 25 of the largest semiconductor companies globally, market-cap weighted, with no caps:

| Top Holdings | SMH Weight | Role in Global Chips |
|---|---|---|
| **Nvidia** | **15.5%** | GPU/AI compute — the single most important chip company |
| **TSMC** | 9.8% | World's largest foundry — makes chips for Nvidia, Apple, AMD |
| **Micron** | 7.3% | DRAM and NAND memory |
| **AMD** | 7.2% | CPUs and GPUs — Nvidia's closest competitor |
| **Intel** | 6.6% | CPUs and foundry — the legacy giant |
| **ASML** | 5.5% | Makes the machines that make chips — extreme ultraviolet lithography |

**What's unique about SMH:**

1. **Global supply chain exposure.** SMH holds TSMC (Taiwan), ASML (Netherlands), and Samsung (Korea) — roughly 20% of the fund is non-US. This captures the semiconductor supply chain's global reality: chips are designed in America, manufactured in Taiwan, using Dutch equipment, with Korean memory.

2. **Extreme Nvidia concentration.** At 15.5%, Nvidia is by far the largest single holding. When AI infrastructure demand surges, Nvidia benefits disproportionately — and SMH captures that.

3. **The long-term winner.** Over 10 years, SMH has returned ~2,300% — more than doubling SOXX's ~2,000%. The concentrated bet on the biggest winners has paid off.

## SOXX: The Diversified Alternative

SOXX tracks the ICE Semiconductor Index (30 stocks) with **individual position caps** that prevent any single name from dominating:

| Top Holdings | SOXX Weight | vs SMH |
|---|---|---|
| **Micron** | ~10% | Higher weight than SMH |
| **AMD** | ~9% | Higher |
| **Broadcom** | ~6.6% | Similar |
| **Intel** | ~6.5% | Similar |
| **Marvell Technology** | ~6.2% | Higher |
| **Applied Materials** | ~6% | Present (equipment) |
| **Lam Research** | ~5% | Present (equipment) |
| **KLA Corp** | ~5% | Present (equipment) |

**What's unique about SOXX:**

1. **More equipment-maker exposure.** Applied Materials, Lam Research, KLA — the companies that make the machinery used to manufacture chips — have significantly higher weights in SOXX. In 2026, as chipmakers ramped capital spending on new fabs, equipment makers outperformed chip designers, driving SOXX's relative outperformance.

2. **Position caps prevent concentration.** No single stock exceeds roughly 10%. You won't get the full Nvidia upside — but you also won't get the full downside if AI capex spending disappoints.

3. **More US-centric.** Less international exposure than SMH. If you want global supply chain diversification, SMH is the better fit.

### What's Driving These Returns

1. **AI capex from Chinese tech giants.** ByteDance, Tencent, Alibaba, and Baidu are spending tens of billions on domestic AI infrastructure. They're buying Chinese chips (Huawei Ascend, Cambricon) rather than Nvidia's restricted A800/H800.

2. **Semiconductor localization push.** US sanctions have forced China to build its own chip equipment supply chain. AMEC (etching), Naura (deposition), and other domestic equipment makers are benefiting from a wave of Chinese fab construction — equipment tender volumes were up 35%+ YoY.

3. **CXMT IPO.** China's top DRAM maker is expected to list on the STAR Market in July 2026, bringing a major new semiconductor name to the index and generating buying pressure.

4. **Narrow, concentrated funds.** The best-performing ETFs are the most focused — semiconductor **equipment** and **materials** rather than broad chips. The narrower the mandate, the bigger the return — and the bigger the risk.

## The Concentration Problem

| | Nvidia Weight | Top 3 Weight | Top 10 Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SMH** | 15.5% | 33% | ~65% |
| **SOXX** | ~5% | 26% | ~55% |
| **S&P 500 (VOO)** | 7.9% | 20% | 37% |

If you already own an S&P 500 ETF, you already have ~8% in Nvidia — more than SOXX does. Adding SMH on top of VOO means you're triple-concentrating: S&P 500 has Nvidia → SMH has 2× more Nvidia → you now have ~20%+ of your portfolio in one stock.

This has been a fantastic trade for five years. Whether it continues to be is the single largest risk question facing semiconductor ETF investors.

## Key Takeaways

1. **SMH is the long-term winner** but carries extreme single-stock concentration. Nvidia at 15.5% means the fund is, effectively, a leveraged bet on AI compute demand continuing to exceed expectations.

2. **SOXX/SOXQ won in 2026** because the semiconductor rally broadened to equipment makers. In a capex cycle, the companies that sell shovels (equipment) can outperform the miners (chip designers).

3. **China semiconductor ETFs have delivered the most extreme returns** — +177% in six months — driven by localization, AI capex, and narrow fund mandates. Global access is limited; the available proxy (KSTR, +72% YTD) captures some but not all of this.

4. **Do you need a standalone semiconductor ETF?** If you own VOO (7.9% Nvidia), you're already making a massive semiconductor bet. Adding SMH or SOXX on top amplifies that concentration. Only do it deliberately.

5. **SOXQ at 0.19% is the best cost option** — nearly half the price of SMH and SOXX for substantially similar exposure.
