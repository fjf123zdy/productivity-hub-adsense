# Gold ETFs 2026: GLD vs IAU vs China Gold ETFs — Which Is the Best Way to Own Gold?

**By ETF Bridge Research** | July 1, 2026 | 9 min read

---

Gold has rallied roughly 70% since the start of 2025, hitting levels that would have seemed improbable just two years ago. Central banks are buying at record pace — the People's Bank of China has added gold for 14 consecutive months. State Street's 2026 outlook argues most investors remain underweight despite the 50% rally. And gold ETFs — the simplest way to own bullion without physical custody — have become a multi-hundred-billion-dollar asset class.

But not all gold ETFs are created equal. US investors face a choice between GLD (0.40%) and much cheaper alternatives (IAU at 0.25%, IAUM at 0.09%). China's gold ETF market offers the cheapest option (0.20%) and the best liquidity in Asia. And for US taxpayers, gold ETF gains are taxed at a punitive "collectibles" rate that can reach 28%.

This article compares the major gold ETFs across both markets — with a focus on what matters most over decades of holding: fees, tracking quality, and tax efficiency.

---

## Why Own Gold in an ETF?

| Method | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Physical Gold** (bars, coins) | No counterparty risk; tangible | Storage costs, insurance, dealer spreads, illiquidity |
| **Gold ETF** (GLD, IAU, 518880) | Instant liquidity, low cost, no storage, no theft risk | Expense ratio, counterparty risk (custodian), tax treatment |
| **Gold Mining ETFs** (GDX, 159562) | Leverage to gold price, dividends | Mining operational risk, doesn't track gold directly |

For most investors, a physical gold ETF is the best balance of cost, convenience, and purity. You own a share of a trust that holds vaulted gold bars — no storage, no insurance, no dealer markup.

### The GLD Premium: What Are You Paying For?

GLD charges 0.40% — more than 4× the cheapest alternative. Why does it still manage $165 billion?

**1. Liquidity.** GLD is the most actively traded gold ETF. Its options chain is the deepest in the precious metals space. If you're trading gold actively — or selling covered calls — GLD is the only game in town.

**2. Institutional infrastructure.** GLD was first (2004). Generations of traders, algorithms, and institutional portfolios are built around it. Switching to IAUM saves 31 basis points but costs institutional systems millions in reconfiguration.

**3. Brand recognition.** For many, GLD simply *is* the gold ETF. The ticker is synonymous with the asset class.

For a buy-and-hold investor, none of these justify a 0.40% fee when IAUM costs 0.09% and holds the identical asset:

| | GLD (0.40%) | IAUM (0.09%) |
|---|---|---|
| **$50,000, 10 years, 5% gold appreciation** | $10,900 in fees | $2,500 in fees |
| **$50,000, 20 years** | $22,800 in fees | $5,100 in fees |

The fee gap alone costs $17,700 on a $50,000 position over 20 years. For identical exposure to identical gold bars in identical vaults.

### Tax Warning: Gold ETFs Are Not Like Stock ETFs

The IRS classifies physical gold ETFs as **collectibles** — not securities. Long-term capital gains are taxed at your marginal rate up to a maximum of **28%**, versus 20% for stocks. This makes gold ETFs one of the most tax-inefficient assets to hold in a taxable account.

| Asset | Maximum Long-Term Capital Gains Rate |
|---|---|
| US Stocks / Stock ETFs | 20% |
| Gold ETFs (GLD, IAU, etc.) | **28%** (collectibles rate) |
| Gold held >1 year in IRA | 0% (tax-deferred growth) |

> **If you hold gold ETFs, hold them in an IRA.** The 28% collectibles rate makes taxable accounts punitive.

### The Two-Tier Fee Structure

China's gold ETF market splits into two tiers:
- **The "Big Four" legacy funds** (Huaan, Bosera, E Fund, Guotai) at 0.60% — older, larger, better liquidity
- **The new low-fee entrants** (China AMC, ICBC, Southern) at 0.20% — 67% cheaper, decent but not best-in-class liquidity

For buy-and-hold investors with Stock Connect access, the 0.20% funds offer the cheapest gold ETF exposure globally — cheaper than even IAUM's 0.09% when adjusted for local considerations.

### The SGE Connection

Chinese gold ETFs track the Shanghai Gold Exchange (SGE) Au99.99 spot price, not the London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) price that GLD/IAU track. The two benchmarks are 99.9% correlated but occasionally diverge during periods of Chinese capital controls or import quota restrictions. The SGE price can trade at a small premium or discount to the international price.

## Gold Mining ETFs: The Amplifier

If physical gold ETFs are the asset, gold mining ETFs are the leveraged bet:

| ETF | Ticker | YTD 2026 | 2025 | What It Holds |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **VanEck Gold Miners** | GDX | ~+30% | ~+18% | Large-cap gold miners (Newmont, Barrick, Agnico Eagle) |
| **中国黄金股ETF (China Gold Stocks)** | 159562 | ~+64% | **+137%** | Chinese gold mining stocks |

Gold miners amplify gold price moves. When gold rises, miners' profits expand faster because extraction costs are relatively fixed. The flipside: when gold falls, miners fall harder. GDX's correlation with gold is ~0.75 — it doesn't track bullion one-to-one.

China's gold stock ETF (159562) returned 137% in 2025 — nearly double gold's own ~58% gain that year. This is leverage by another name: operational leverage embedded in mining company earnings.

> **Gold mining ETFs are not a gold substitute.** They're a bet on gold *plus* management skill, geopolitical risk (where mines are located), and operational execution. Use them to amplify a gold view, not to replace a bullion allocation.

## Key Takeaways

1. **IAUM at 0.09% is the cheapest gold ETF in the world.** Paying GLD's 0.40% when IAUM exists costs you $17,700 per $50,000 over 20 years — for the same gold bars in the same vaults.

2. **China's 0.20% gold ETFs are globally competitive on cost** — 67% cheaper than the legacy Huaan fund — but require Stock Connect or onshore access.

3. **Gold ETF gains are taxed at 28%.** Hold them in an IRA or tax-advantaged account whenever possible. This is the single most overlooked cost in gold ETF investing.

4. **Gold mining ETFs are not gold substitutes.** They amplify gold moves — in both directions — and introduce operational risks gold bullion doesn't have.

5. **Central bank buying is structural.** The PBOC's 14 consecutive months of gold purchases are part of a global trend of central banks diversifying reserves away from US Treasuries. This is a multi-year, not a cyclical, driver.
